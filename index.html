Dad Tax Act 2024

Act No. 42 of 2024 as amended

An Act relating to the taxation of confectionery, chocolates, and snack foods entering the household, and for related purposes

âš  MANDATORY COMPLIANCE REQUIRED

All households are required to comply with Dad Tax provisions effective immediately

Status: In Force

This legislation came into effect on the date of Royal Assent and applies to all Australian households where a Dad is present.

Part 1 â€” Preliminary

Section 1: Short Title

This Act may be cited as the Dad Tax Act 2024.

Section 2: Commencement

This Act commences on the day it receives Royal Assent and applies retrospectively to all previous instances of dads consuming children's snacks.

Section 3: Definitions

In this Act, unless the contrary intention appears:

  • "Dad" means any paternal figure, including fathers, stepfathers, grandfathers, or persons acting in a paternal capacity within a household;
  • "Taxable Goods" means any chocolate, lollies, chips, biscuits, ice cream, or other confectionery or snack foods;
  • "Enter the Household" means the act of crossing the threshold of the primary residence, whether through the front door, back door, or any other point of entry;
  • "Small Portion" means a reasonable amount as determined solely by the Dad, generally interpreted as between 10-30% of the total goods;
  • "Children" means any offspring, stepchildren, grandchildren, or young persons under the care of the Dad;
  • "Immediate" means within a reasonable timeframe, not exceeding 2 hours from the time of entry into the household.

Part 2 â€” Imposition of Dad Tax

Section 4: Liability to Dad Tax

4(1)

A Dad Tax is hereby imposed on all Taxable Goods that enter the household.

4(2)

The Dad Tax must be collected immediately upon the Taxable Goods entering the household, or at such time as the Dad becomes aware of their presence.

4(3)

No person may consume Taxable Goods prior to the Dad Tax being collected, except with express written consent from the Dad.

Section 5: Mandatory Purposes of Dad Tax

The Dad Tax serves three (3) essential purposes, each of which is mandatory:

5(1) Purpose One: Safety Testing

The Dad must consume a Small Portion of all Taxable Goods to ensure that:

  • The goods are not poisoned, contaminated, or otherwise unsafe for consumption;
  • The goods meet minimum quality standards as determined by the Dad;
  • The goods are suitable for consumption by Children;
  • No foreign objects or hazardous materials are present in the goods.

Note: This safety testing function is crucial and cannot be delegated or waived.

5(2) Purpose Two: Paternal Nutrition

The Dad Tax ensures that:

  • The Dad receives adequate sustenance and remains properly fed;
  • The Dad's blood sugar levels are maintained at optimal levels for household operations;
  • The Dad's happiness and wellbeing are preserved through the consumption of treats;
  • The Dad has sufficient energy to perform paternal duties including, but not limited to: fixing things, giving piggyback rides, and telling dad jokes.

Note: A happy Dad is essential to the proper functioning of the household.

5(3) Purpose Three: Educational Function

The Dad Tax provides essential education to Children regarding:

  • The existence and operation of taxation systems in society;
  • The principle that nothing in life is free;
  • The concept of governmental (or paternal) authority;
  • The understanding that contributing to the greater good (keeping Dad happy) benefits everyone;
  • Preparation for real-world taxation that they will encounter as adults;
  • The importance of sharing and thinking of others.

Note: This educational function is recognised as a valuable life lesson and character-building exercise.

Section 6: Rate of Tax

6(1)

The standard rate of Dad Tax shall be a Small Portion as defined in Section 3, with the exact amount to be determined by the Dad at his sole discretion.

6(2)

The Dad may apply a progressive tax rate, taking into account factors including but not limited to:

  • The Dad's level of hunger;
  • The desirability of the Taxable Goods;
  • The quantity of Taxable Goods available;
  • Recent behaviour of the Children;
  • Whether it's been a long day;
  • The Dad's favourite snacks receiving a higher rate of taxation.

Part 3 â€” Compliance and Enforcement

Section 7: Obligations of Children

7(1)

Children must present all Taxable Goods to the Dad immediately upon entering the household.

7(2)

Children must not hide, conceal, or otherwise attempt to evade the Dad Tax.

7(3)

Children must wait patiently while the Dad conducts the necessary safety testing.

Section 8: Exemptions

8(1)

The following Taxable Goods may be exempt from Dad Tax at the Dad's discretion:

  • Goods purchased for special occasions (birthdays, holidays) - subject to a reduced rate;
  • Goods that the Dad finds unpalatable or undesirable;
  • Goods that have been specifically pre-approved as exempt by the Dad in writing.
8(2)

All exemptions must be approved by the Dad and may be revoked at any time without notice.

Section 9: Penalties for Non-Compliance

Warning: Penalties Apply

9(1)

Failure to comply with the Dad Tax provisions may result in:

  • Confiscation of all remaining Taxable Goods;
  • A lecture about respect and following rules;
  • Increased scrutiny of future Taxable Goods entering the household;
  • Temporary suspension of privileges;
  • Disappointed dad face;
  • The phrase "I'm not angry, I'm just disappointed."
9(2)

Repeated or flagrant violations may result in the Dad eating ALL the good snacks.

Section 10: Appeals Process

10(1)

Children may appeal decisions regarding Dad Tax to the Mum, however:

  • The Mum may uphold the Dad Tax;
  • The Mum may impose additional penalties;
  • The Mum may institute a Mum Tax in addition to the Dad Tax;
  • Appeals are not guaranteed to be successful.
10(2)

The Dad's decision is final in all matters relating to taxation rates and safety testing procedures.

Part 4 â€” Miscellaneous Provisions

Section 11: Multiple Dads

11(1)

In households with multiple persons qualifying as Dads, each Dad is entitled to collect Dad Tax independently.

11(2)

Dads may coordinate to avoid double taxation if they so choose, but are not required to do so.

Section 12: Visiting Friends' Houses

12(1)

When visiting another household, the host Dad's taxation authority takes precedence.

12(2)

Children should not expect exemptions when visiting friends' houses, as Dad Tax is universally applied across all Australian households.

Section 13: Sunset Provisions

13(1)

This Act shall remain in force in perpetuity.

13(2)

The Act shall continue to apply even after Children reach adulthood, when visiting the family home.

13(3)

Upon becoming Dads themselves, former Children gain the authority to impose Dad Tax in their own households, thus perpetuating the sacred tradition.
